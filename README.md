Project Description

This project analyzes bank customer data to understand why customers leave the bank (customer churn). The goal is to identify patterns and factors that influence customer churn so businesses can improve customer retention.

Objectives

Explore the bank customer dataset.

Identify factors that influence customer churn.

Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA).

Create visualizations to understand customer behavior.

Tools & Technologies

Python

Pandas

NumPy

Matplotlib

Seaborn

Google Colaboratory

PowerBI for Data visuvalizations

Dataset Features

The dataset includes the following information about customers:

Credit Score

Geography

Gender

Age

Tenure

Balance

Number of Products

Has Credit Card

Is Active Member

Estimated Salary

Churn (Target Variable)

Analysis Performed

During the analysis, the following steps were completed:

Data cleaning and preprocessing

Checking missing values

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Data visualization

Identifying patterns related to churn

Key insights :
25% of customers have zero balance, indicating a significant portion of inactive or low-deposit accounts.
The presence of many zero-balance customers may indicate:
Inactive or low-engagement customers
Customers using the account for limited purposes
Potential churn risk behavior
Customers with higher balances may be more valuable
Customers with zero or low balances may be more likely to churn

Insights :

Inactive customers have a higher churn rate.

Customers with higher balances are more likely to leave.

Certain regions show higher churn.

Recommendation :

Improve engagement with inactive customers.

Offer loyalty programs for high-value customers.

Conclusion :

The analysis helps understand customer behavior and highlights key factors that may lead to customer churn. These insights can help banks take actions to improve customer satisfaction and reduce churn.
